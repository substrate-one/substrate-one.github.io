### Why we exist

We’re building something ambitious with a small team. Our advantage is focus, speed, and taste. We win by finding the best ideas and executing them relentlessly.

---

### What we believe

* **Best ideas win:** Titles don’t. Seniority doesn’t. The clearest thinking does.
* **Initiative is the default:** If you see a problem or an opportunity, you own it.
* **Leadership is a behavior:** Anyone can lead by setting direction, unblocking others, and raising the bar.
* **Velocity beats perfection:** We iterate fast, measure impact, and improve continuously.
* **Remote-first is real:** We optimize for distributed work, written communication, and inclusive collaboration across time zones.

---

### What “great” looks like here

* **You ship:** You turn ambiguity into a plan, a prototype, a release, and a measurable result.
* **You think like an owner:** You optimize for the company’s outcomes, not your local preferences.
* **You’re high-agency:** You don’t wait to be told. You make the call, and you pull in others when needed.
* **You communicate clearly:** You write, you document, and you keep others aligned without drama.
* **You raise the quality bar:** You improve systems, tests, observability, and developer experience as you go.
* **You learn fast:** You seek feedback early, change your mind quickly, and share what you learn.

---

### How we make decisions

* **Disagree and commit:** Debate hard, then align and execute.
* **Strong opinions, loosely held:** Bring a view, update it with evidence.
* **Default to action:** If reversible, decide quickly. If irreversible, slow down and get input.
* **Data + judgment:** Metrics matter, but so does product sense and user empathy.

---

### How we work (remote-first norms)

* **Async by default:** Write things down: proposals, decisions, status, and context.
* **Meetings are expensive:** Use them for high-bandwidth topics. Always have an agenda and outcomes.
* **Time zones are respected:** No expectation of instant replies. Use “when you wake up” workflows.
* **Transparent work:** Progress lives in shared docs, tickets, PRs, and decision notes—not in DMs.

---

### Execution principles

* **Small batches:** Ship thin slices that deliver user value.
* **Fast feedback loops:** Prototype early, demo often, measure impact.
* **The Path:** Make it work → make it right → make it fast. But don’t skip “right” forever.
* **Efficiency:** Automate and simplify. Kill repeated toil. Fix root causes.

---

### Engineering values

* **Production is a feature:** Reliability, security, and privacy are part of shipping.
* **Pragmatic excellence:** Clean code, good tests, and good monitoring—scaled to the stage.
* **Readable > clever:** Optimize for maintainability and shared understanding.
* **Own what you build:** You ship it, you support it, you improve it.

---

### Collaboration and feedback

* **Candor with care:** Direct feedback, no ego, assume good intent.
* **No hero culture:** We build systems that don’t rely on 2am rescues.
* **Credit is shared:** We celebrate team outcomes, not individual theatrics.

---

### What we don’t do

* **No politics:** Influence comes from clarity and results.
* **No gatekeeping:** Knowledge is documented and shared.
* **No waiting for permission:** For small, reversible improvements.
* **No status games:** Titles are for org charts; impact is what matters.

---

### What you can expect

* High trust and high standards
* Autonomy with accountability
* A team that moves fast, learns faster, and cares about craft
* A culture where you can lead from day one

### Our simple rule

> **Bring the best idea you can, ship it quickly, measure what happened, and make it better.**
